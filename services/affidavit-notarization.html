Affidavits & Statutory Declarations Notarization Services

An affidavit or statutory declaration is a sworn statement that can be used as evidence in legal proceedings. Our professional notaries are experienced in witnessing and notarizing affidavits for immigration, family law disputes, property matters, and other legal situations.

What is an Affidavit or Statutory Declaration?

A statutory declaration is a formal written statement made by a person (the deponent) who declares that the contents are true to the best of their knowledge. In Ontario, statutory declarations are often used instead of sworn affidavits and carry similar legal weight. An affidavit is a sworn statement that is legally binding.

Common Uses for Affidavits & Statutory Declarations

  • Immigration & Travel: Supporting immigration applications, sponsorships, travel letters, and visa applications
  • Family Law: Child custody disputes, support modifications, and family court proceedings
  • Property & Real Estate: Property disputes, ownership verification, and boundary claims
  • Legal Proceedings: Evidence for civil lawsuits and other legal matters
  • Professional Licenses: Character references and professional credibility statements
  • Financial & Banking: Verification of identity and financial status for banking matters
